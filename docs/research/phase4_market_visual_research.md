## Observed market patterns

- Successful survivor games make the player, enemies, pickups, and hazard zones readable before decorative fidelity.
- Strong entries use environment identity to support the loop: caves/mining, dark fantasy halls, top-down arenas, or readable item-heavy rooms.
- Fast runs and repeated upgrades are usually paired with clear short-term goals and high-contrast feedback.
- Maps that look rich still avoid making small loot or enemies disappear under high-frequency floor noise.
- A visually distinct biome set is useful only when it reinforces navigation, danger, and reward expectations.

## Phase 4 visual direction

- Keep the unique value on exploration, crystals, danger rewards, and build completion.
- Push the environment from flat grid color toward crystal labyrinth art, but reserve the brightest contrast for pickups, enemies, boss warnings, and UI.
- Use four clear biome identities:
  - 星屑平原: cyan star-crystal floor and readable safe baseline.
  - 紫晶の森: purple crystals and roots, but with darker floor centers.
  - 赤熱鉱床: red ore and mine rails, with danger accent kept below warning UI brightness.
  - 虚無領域: dark cosmic void, strong boundary language, sparse high-value glow.
- Treat generated art as unapproved until human review updates the manifest.
